Trail Story

My name is Jorge Acosta, and this is an introduction to my journey.

For a long time, I've always waited for the "right time" to start something. I remember when I was 13 years old, I really wanted to play for FC Dallas DA, which was the highest level of youth soccer at the time. Every day I would check the FC Dallas youth website to see when tryouts were, who to contact, and what it would take. I watched their games on YouTube and told myself, one day I'll be on that team.

But when the next day came, I wouldn't train. I wouldn't go on a run. Not because I didn't want it, but because I wasn't obsessed with it. I didn't want it as badly as I told myself I did. I kept pushing things off, telling myself I'd start tomorrow. Every now and then I would train, but never consistently enough to actually take myself to the next level.

Looking back, that mindset didn't stop there. It followed me into other parts of my life. Whether it was asking a girl out, starting a business idea, or taking a risk on something new, I kept waiting for the perfect moment.

At some point, I realized how short life really is. Even at 20 years old, it started to feel like I was wasting time. I got tired of hearing excusesâ€”from myself, from friends, from family. Life doesn't pause or wait for anyone to feel ready, and it definitely wasn't going to wait for me.

Identity

This past summer the idea came to me to be spontaneous and adventurous. I wanted to be the friend you see on TikTok and Snapchat, going on the most chaotic adventures. Which got me thinking, is it because I want to do it for myself or to show other people what I'm doing? Well, it's both. I want to explore my journey with the world, to show people who they can become through me.

I don't really have it all figured out. I'm just trying things, learning along the way, and doing more instead of sitting around thinking about it. I want people to see my journey and feel like they can do it too, even if they don't know exactly where they're headed.

Upcoming

What's up and coming for me is getting out there more. My first destination will be Oregon on my birthday a couple months from now.

This summer is about getting back to the things I love and trying new ones: surfing again, rock climbing, and maybe even skydiving (still scares me, so we'll see).

I also want to hit more soccer games, especially in Europe and South America, and feel the sport where it means everything to people. No strict plan just keep moving.
